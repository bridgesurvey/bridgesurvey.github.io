Dwarf Galaxy

The central stellar component of dwarf galaxies, where star formation occurs and stellar feedback originates.

BRIDGES targets the circumgalactic medium around these low-mass systems to understand baryon cycling.

Gas Recycling

The complex process by which gas is expelled from the galaxy and later re-accreted, enriching the circumgalactic medium.

This recycling process is fundamental to understanding how dwarf galaxies evolve over cosmic time.

Stellar Feedback Outflows

Hot, metal-enriched gas driven out by supernovae and stellar winds from the central galaxy.

These outflows redistribute metals and regulate star formation efficiency.

Circumgalactic Medium

The extended gaseous atmosphere surrounding the galaxy, containing a significant fraction of the halo's baryons.

The CGM serves as the interface between the galaxy and the cosmic web, mediating gas flows.

Pristine Gas Inflows

Cold, metal-poor gas streams feeding the galaxy from the cosmic web and intergalactic medium.

These inflows provide the raw material for continued star formation in dwarf systems.

About the BRIDGES Survey

The BRIDGES survey represents a comprehensive observational campaign to map and characterize the circumgalactic medium (CGM) around dwarf galaxies. By studying these low-mass systems, we gain crucial insights into the fundamental processes that govern baryon cycling in the universe.

Dwarf galaxies serve as ideal laboratories for understanding feedback mechanisms because their shallow potential wells make them highly sensitive to stellar feedback processes. The interplay between gas inflows, star formation, and outflows in these systems provides a window into the physics that shaped galaxy evolution throughout cosmic time.

Using multi-wavelength observations spanning from UV to radio wavelengths, BRIDGES traces the complete baryon cycle: from pristine gas accretion through star formation to metal-enriched outflows and the eventual recycling of processed material back into the galaxy.

Map CGM Structure

Characterize the spatial distribution and kinematics of gas around dwarf galaxies using absorption line spectroscopy and emission mapping.

Quantify Baryon Budget

Measure the mass and metallicity of gas in different phases to understand how efficiently dwarf galaxies retain their baryons.

Constrain Feedback Models

Provide observational benchmarks for theoretical models of stellar feedback and its impact on galaxy evolution.

Cosmic Context

Connect dwarf galaxy observations to broader questions about reionization, chemical evolution, and structure formation.
